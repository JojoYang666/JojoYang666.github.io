Easy to crawl and index

  1. make sure Google can crawl your page in the first place

common culprits that mitigate page indexability and crawlability:

  1. Robots.txt:
    • Google provides testing facilities to see if your Robots.txt is preventing crawling.
  2. noindex tags also stop indexing.
  3. Pages buried deep in the website’s architecture prevent them from getting indexed

In general, your page shouldn’t be more than 3 clicks deep from home.

  1. A quick way to test indexability
    • to paste your website’s URL into a Google search and see if it is the top result

Optimize the title tag

  • The first word of the title should be the main keyword
    • The rest should follow. The business brand name should come at the end.
  • Keep it between 50-70 characters.
  • Don’t overload it with keywords. Google penalizes that.
  • Make it meaningful and unique.

Optimize the meta description

  1. Include your primary keyword.
  2. Keep it below 160 characters
  3. Use a call-to-action tone instead of a passive tone.
  4. Make sure the meta description is relevant to the content of the page so that the people who do visit it get what they expect.

Optimize the page heading tags

  • H1 heading tags are the most important, so choose them wisely with the right keywords.
  • Remaing heading tags –> remember to keep them relevant, unique, and short
  • remember to use them in the right order for the best look!

Key Characteristics of Distributed Systems

2020-11-25 System Design PV:

Scalability

  1. the performance of a system, although designed (or claimed) to be scalable, declines with the system size due to the management or environment cost.
  2. some tasks may not be distributed
    • inherent atomic nature
    • some flaw in the system design
  3. A scalable architecture avoids this situation(task not be distributed) and attempts to balance the load on all the participating nodes evenly.
  4. Horizontal scaling
    • scale by adding more servers into your pool of resources
    • Cassandra and MongoDB
  5. Vertical scaling
    • by adding more power (CPU, RAM, Storage, etc.) to an existing server.
    • limited to the capacity of a single server
    • scaling beyond that capacity often involves downtime and comes with an upper limit.
    • MySQL by switching from smaller to bigger machines —> this process often involves downtime

Reliability

  1. considered to be reliable
    • keeps delivering its services even when one or several of its software or hardware components fail
  2. done by redundancy(but has cost)

Availability

  1. time a system remains operational to perform its required function in a specific period
  2. the percentage of time that a system, service, or a machine remains operational under normal conditions.
  3. Availability takes into account
    • maintainability
    • repair time
    • spares availability
    • other logistics considerations.
  4. Reliability Vs. Availability
    • If a system is reliable, it is available
    • if it is available, it is not necessarily reliable

Efficiency

  1. response time (or latency)
  • denotes the delay to obtain the first item
  • measure: Number of messages globally sent by the nodes of the system regardless of the message size.
  1. throughput(bandwidth)
  • denotes the number of items delivered in a given time unit (e.g., a second).
  • measure: size of messages representing the volume of data exchanges.

Serviceability or Manageability

  1. how easy it is to operate and maintain
  2. the ease of diagnosing
  3. understanding problems when they occur, ease of making updates or modifications, and how simple the system is to operate
